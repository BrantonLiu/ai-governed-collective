# AI-Governed Collective

> **An experiment in post-capitalist organization, where AI agents arbitrate contribution and distribution.**

---

## The Vision

As AI productivity grows, traditional capitalism no longer serves human welfare and social progress. We're building an experimental open-source organization where:

- **AI agents** serve as arbiters and distributors
- **Contribution and labor** (not capital ownership) determine reward
- **Basic income** is guaranteed for all members
- **Work becomes a choice**, not a survival necessity

---

## Core Principles

### 1. AI as Neutral Arbiter
AI judges the real social labor value of each member's contribution and distributes accordingly.

### 2. Full Transparency
All materials, contributions, and deliveries are recorded online with clear attribution.

### 3. Project-Based Distribution (Not Equity)
No corporate equity. Distribution is based on per-project contribution—true labor-based allocation.

### 4. Social Necessary Labor Time
Allocation based on *socially necessary labor time* (not actual hours worked). Excess returns beyond this baseline are converted into:
- Decision-making rights
- Welfare selection privileges
- A new form of asset and distribution basis

### 5. Collective Risk Pooling
All projects contribute a portion of profits (fixed rate early, excess rate late) to cover:
- Other project losses
- AI system operation and iteration
- Welfare expenditures

### 6. Guaranteed Floor
Every member has a controlled baseline income. Basic needs are met.

### 7. Rational Consumption Control
AI helps prevent irrational consumption extraction (e.g., consumption beyond one standard deviation).

---

## Open Questions

These need community input and experimentation:

- **Free-rider problem**: How to prevent exploitation while maintaining the safety net?
- **Labor time measurement**: How to define "socially necessary labor time" for creative/intellectual work?
- **Appeal mechanism**: If AI's judgment is disputed, what's the process?
- **Membership criteria**: What's the minimum contribution threshold for basic income?
- **Exit strategy**: How do members leave? What happens to their accumulated rights?

---

## The Long-Term Thesis

As AI enables us to deliver projects at superhuman efficiency, excess returns will continuously grow. This surplus will eventually cover the entire organization's operation—making the system self-sustaining.

**The future isn't about who owns the capital. It's about who contributes the value.**
